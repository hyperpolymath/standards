## üö® Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to:

**Contact**: See `.well-known/security.txt` for current contact information

**What to Include**:
1. Your contact information
2. Names (real, usernames, pseudonyms) of individuals involved
3. Description of the incident
4. Links to public evidence (if applicable)
5. Any additional context

**Confidentiality**: All complaints will be reviewed and investigated **confidentially**.

### Response Timeline

| Timeline     | Action                                |
|------------- |---------------------------------------|
| **24 hours** | Acknowledgement of report             |
| **72 hours** | Preliminary assessment                |
| **7 days**   | Determination and action (if needed)  |

### Enforcement Guidelines

We follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

#### 1. Correction (Minor Issue)

**Community Impact**: Inappropriate language or unprofessional behaviour.

**Consequence**: Private written warning, clarification of why the behaviour was inappropriate, and request for apology.

#### 2. Warning (Moderate Issue)

**Community Impact**: Violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: Warning with consequences for continued behaviour. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. Violation may result in temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban (Serious Issue)

**Community Impact**: Serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behaviour.

**Consequence**: Temporary ban from all project interaction and public communication for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved during this period.

#### 4. Permanent Ban (Severe Issue)

**Community Impact**: Pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression toward individuals or groups.

**Consequence**: Permanent ban from all public project interaction.

## üîÑ Reversibility & Redemption

In keeping with RSR principles of **reversibility and emotional safety**:

- **Mistakes are learning opportunities**: We assume good faith and provide chances to correct behaviour
- **Bans are reversible**: After demonstrating changed behaviour and understanding, individuals may appeal
- **Growth is valued**: We recognise that people can change and improve

**Appeal Process**:
1. Wait a minimum cooling-off period (30 days for temporary bans)
2. Submit appeal via email (see `.well-known/security.txt`)
3. Demonstrate understanding of what went wrong
4. Outline steps taken to prevent recurrence
5. Community review of appeal

- **Cool-headed decisions**: Evidence-based, not reactionary
- **Graduated trust**: TPCF framework (Perimeter 3 ‚Üí 2 ‚Üí 1)

# Legal Resources - Palimpsest License

## Overview

This directory contains comprehensive legal resources for the Palimpsest License project, including pro bono legal organisation contacts, engagement letters, legal strategy documents, and jurisdictional frameworks.

**Primary Purpose:** Support legal validation, policy advocacy, and enforcement of the Palimpsest License across multiple jurisdictions.

**Primary Jurisdiction:** Netherlands (Dutch law governs)
**Secondary Jurisdictions:** United Kingdom (England & Wales, Scotland), European Union, United States

## Usage Guidelines

### Before Contacting Organisations

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Read relevant organisation profile (e.g., `/legal/pro-bono/organisations/Netherlands-organisations.md`)
- [ ] Review priority contacts assessment (`/legal/pro-bono/contacts/priority-contacts.md`)
- [ ] Select appropriate engagement letter template
- [ ] Customise template thoroughly (show you've researched them)
- [ ] Prepare attachments (Palimpsest v0.4, one-pager, specific questions)
- [ ] Review template's pre-send checklist
- [ ] Update master database with "Contacted" status and date

### After Receiving Response

**Track Progress:**
- [ ] Update `/legal/pro-bono/contacts/master-database.md` with:
  - Response date
  - Outcome (interested/declined/referred/no response)
  - Next steps
  - Key contact person
- [ ] Follow up according to template guidelines
- [ ] Document any legal opinions, feedback, or guidance received
- [ ] Thank respondents promptly and professionally

### For Policy Advocacy

**Resources to Use:**
- Legal strategy document (policy advocacy section)
- Honest broker candidates (for credible voices)
- Organisation contacts (policy-focused orgs)
- Jurisdictional frameworks (inform policy positions)

**Best Practices:**
- Evidence-based arguments (cite legal analysis, creator harm)
- Specific policy proposals (not vague advocacy)
- Coalition building (joint submissions stronger than solo)
- Timing (align with consultations, legislative calendars)

## Security & Confidentiality

### Public vs. Private Information

**PUBLIC (can be shared):**
- Organisation profiles (based on public information)
- Engagement letter templates (generic)
- Legal strategy (general analysis)
- Jurisdictional frameworks

**CONFIDENTIAL (Palimpsest Council only):**
- Master database (strategic outreach information)
- Priority contacts (competitive intelligence)
- Specific outreach letters (once sent, may contain sensitive info)
- Legal opinions received (unless authors approve public sharing)
- Advisory Council deliberations

**PROTECT:**
- Personal email addresses of individuals (use professionally, don't share publicly)
- Confidential communications with lawyers (attorney-client privilege if applicable)
- Strategic litigation plans (if test cases emerge)

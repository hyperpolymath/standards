### Project Summary

The Palimpsest License is a layered licensing framework designed to protect creators from AI exploitation whilst maintaining principles of remix culture and legitimate reuse:

**Primary Legal Jurisdiction:** Netherlands (Auteurswet governs)
**Current Version:** v0.4 (in development)
**Governance:** Palimpsest Stewardship Council (multi-stakeholder model)

**Key Innovations:**
1. **AI Training Consent (Clause 1.2):** Requires explicit permission for "non-interpretive" AI systems (those generating creative derivatives)
2. **Metadata Preservation (Clause 2.3):** Mandatory retention of creator context and attribution
3. **Emotional Lineage Protection:** Extends beyond legal attribution to protect narrative intent and cultural context
4. **Quantum-Proof Traceability:** Future-proofing for emerging attribution technologies

### Specific Legal Questions for IViR

We would be immensely grateful for IViR's input on:

#### A. **Auteurswet Compliance**

**1. Moral Rights (Article 25 Auteurswet)**
- Does Palimpsest's "emotional lineage" concept extend Article 25's paternity and integrity rights?
- Are our metadata preservation requirements consistent with Dutch moral rights doctrine?
- Can moral rights be waived or licensed under Dutch law in the manner Palimpsest proposes?

**2. Text and Data Mining Exception (Articles 45c-45h Auteurswet)**
- Does Palimpsest's "opt-in for non-interpretive AI" comply with Article 45d's opt-out mechanism?
- Can we require more restrictive consent than the TDM exception allows?
- What constitutes "machine-readable" opt-out under Dutch implementation?

**3. Economic Rights (Articles 1, 12-13 Auteurswet)**
- Are our derivative work restrictions (requiring consent for AI-generated derivatives) enforceable?
- Does Dutch law recognise "AI-generated derivative" as distinct from human-created derivative?
- Can we distinguish between "transformative" and "non-transformative" uses for licensing purposes?

#### B. **Contract Law Validity (Burgerlijk Wetboek)**

**4. Contractual Enforceability**
- Are Palimpsest's licensing terms valid contracts under Dutch civil law?
- Can we enforce metadata preservation requirements against third parties (platforms)?
- What jurisdiction/choice of law clauses ensure Dutch law governs internationally?

**5. Standard Form Contracts**
- Does Palimpsest risk classification as unfair standard terms (oneerlijke bedingen)?
- Are our restrictions proportionate and reasonable under Dutch contract law?

#### C. **EU Law Alignment**

**6. DSM Directive Transposition**
- Does Netherlands' implementation of Article 17 (platform liability) interact with Palimpsest?
- Can platforms be required to enforce Palimpsest's metadata preservation?
- How does Article 15 (press publishers' rights) inform our approach?

**7. AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)**
- When AI Act applies (2025-2026), how will transparency requirements (Article 53) interact with Palimpsest's AI consent clauses?
- Can Palimpsest's traceability requirements complement AI Act obligations?

#### D. **Enforceability & Practical Implementation**

**8. Cross-Border Enforcement**
- How enforceable is a Dutch law license in other EU jurisdictions? UK? US?
- Should we create jurisdiction-specific versions or rely on conflicts of law principles?

**9. Platform Compliance**
- Can we legally require platforms (e.g., GitHub, Medium, Substack) to respect Palimpsest metadata?
- What technical/legal mechanisms ensure platforms don't strip licensing information?

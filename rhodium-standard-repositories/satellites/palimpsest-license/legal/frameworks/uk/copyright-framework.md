## 2. Copyright Subsistence and Ownership

### 2.1 Works Protected (CDPA 1988, s 1)

Original works in the following categories:
1. **Literary works** (s 3)
   - Written works, computer programmes, databases
   - Includes preparatory design material
2. **Dramatic works** (s 3)
3. **Musical works** (s 3)
4. **Artistic works** (s 4)
   - Graphics, photographs, sculpture, collage
   - Works of architecture, works of artistic craftsmanship

### 2.2 Originality Requirement

**Standard:** *Infopaq International A/S v Danske Dagblades Forening* [2009] EUECJ C-5/08
- Author's own intellectual creation
- Reflects author's personality
- Involves creative choices

**UK Application:** *Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Meltwater Holding BV* [2011] EWCA Civ 890
- Harmonised EU standard applies
- Skill, labour, and judgment insufficient alone

### 2.3 First Ownership (CDPA 1988, s 11)

**General Rule:** Author is first owner of copyright

**Exceptions:**
1. **Employment:** Employer owns copyright in works created in course of employment (s 11(2))
   - *Stevenson Jordan & Harrison Ltd v MacDonald & Evans* [1952] 1 TLR 101
2. **Commissioned Works:** No automatic transfer to commissioning party
   - *Robin Ray v Classic FM plc* [1998] FSR 622

### 2.4 Duration (CDPA 1988, ss 12-15)

- **Literary, dramatic, musical, artistic:** Life of author + 70 years
- **Computer-generated works:** 50 years from creation
- **Sound recordings:** 70 years from publication
- **Films:** 70 years from death of last principal director, author, composer

## 3. Moral Rights

### 3.1 Paternity Right (CDPA 1988, s 77)

**Right to be Identified as Author**
- Applies to literary, dramatic, musical, artistic works and films
- Must be asserted (s 78)
- Exceptions: computer programmes, typefaces, computer-generated works

**Assertion Methods:**
- In assignment/licence instrument
- By notice to copyright owner
- Public assertion in work itself

### 3.2 Integrity Right (CDPA 1988, s 80)

**Right to Object to Derogatory Treatment**
- Treatment = addition, deletion, alteration, adaptation
- Derogatory = distortion, mutilation, prejudicial to honour/reputation

**Relevance to Palimpsest:**
- Protection against metadata stripping
- Protection of "emotional lineage"
- Contextual integrity of symbolic content

**Key Cases:**
- *Tidy v Trustees of Natural History Museum* [1996] EWHC Patents 5
- *Confetti Records v Warner Music UK Ltd* [2003] EWHC 1274 (Ch)
- *Pasterfield v Denham* [1999] FSR 168

### 3.3 False Attribution (CDPA 1988, s 84)

Right not to have work falsely attributed

### 3.4 Privacy Right (CDPA 1988, s 85)

Right to privacy in commissioned photographs and films

### 3.5 Inalienability and Duration

- **Cannot be assigned** (s 94) but can be waived
- **Duration:** Same as copyright except false attribution (20 years after death)
- **Post-mortem enforcement:** Personal representatives may enforce

## 4. Restricted Acts and Infringement

### 4.1 Primary Infringement (CDPA 1988, ss 16-21)

Acts restricted by copyright:
1. **Copying** (s 17) — reproduction in material form
2. **Issuing copies to public** (s 18)
3. **Rental or lending** (s 18A)
4. **Performance, showing or playing in public** (s 19)
5. **Communication to the public** (s 20)
6. **Making adaptation** (s 21)

**Substantial Part Test:** *Infopaq* test; qualitative, not merely quantitative
- *Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Meltwater* [2011] EWCA Civ 890

### 4.2 Secondary Infringement (CDPA 1988, ss 22-26)

Requires knowledge or reason to believe dealing with infringing copy:
- Importing infringing copy
- Possessing or dealing with infringing copy in course of business
- Providing means for making infringing copies
- Permitting use of premises for infringing performance

### 4.3 Authorisation (CDPA 1988, s 16(2))

Liability for authorising infringement:
- *CBS Songs Ltd v Amstrad Consumer Electronics plc* [1988] AC 1013
- *Twentieth Century Fox Film Corp v Newzbin Ltd* [2010] EWHC 608 (Ch)

## 5. Exceptions and Limitations

### 5.1 Fair Dealing (CDPA 1988, ss 29-30)

**Fair dealing for:**
1. **Research and private study** (s 29)
   - Non-commercial research only (s 29(1C))
   - Sufficient acknowledgement required
2. **Criticism, review, and quotation** (s 30)
   - Work must have been made available to public
   - Sufficient acknowledgement
   - Extent must be proportionate

**Fair Dealing Test:** *Newspaper Licensing Agency Ltd v Marks & Spencer plc* [2001] UKHL 38
- Purpose of use
- Nature of work
- Amount taken
- Effect on market

### 5.2 Parody, Caricature, and Pastiche (CDPA 1988, s 30A)

Introduced 2014; no requirement for acknowledgement

**Scope:** *Pelham GmbH v Hütter* [2019] EUECJ C-476/17
- Must evoke existing work whilst being noticeably different
- Must constitute expression of humour or mockery

### 5.3 Text and Data Mining (CDPA 1988, ss 29A, 29B)

**Section 29A:** Non-commercial research (2014)
**Section 29B:** Commercial TDM with lawful access (expected post-DSM implementation)

**Relevance to Palimpsest AI Provisions:**
- Current UK law more restrictive than EU DSM Directive
- Potential conflict with Palimpsest Clause 1.2 (Non-Interpretive systems)
- Contractual override may be possible (s 29B(2))

### 5.4 Disability Exceptions (CDPA 1988, ss 31A-31F)

Accessible format copying for disabled persons

## 6. AI and Computational Use

### 6.1 Current Legislative Position

**Limited Statutory Guidance:**
- Computer-generated works recognised (s 9(3), s 178)
- TDM exceptions limited (ss 29A-29B)
- No specific AI training exception

**Author of Computer-Generated Work:** Person making arrangements necessary for creation (s 9(3))

### 6.2 Judicial Developments

**Interpretive AI Systems:**
- No binding precedent on generative AI
- Potential analogies: derivative works, substantial part, transformative use

**Training Data:**
- *Google LLC v Oracle America Inc* 593 US ___ (2021) [US Supreme Court; persuasive]
- Copying for TDM may constitute reproduction (s 17)

### 6.3 Palimpsest License Provisions

**Clause 1.2 Compatibility:**
- Contractual restriction on Non-Interpretive AI use
- More restrictive than statutory TDM exceptions
- Enforceable as contract term (privity, consideration)

**Clause 2.3 Metadata Preservation:**
- Supports moral rights (integrity)
- May engage CDPA s 80 (derogatory treatment)

### 6.4 Proposed Reforms

**UK Government Consultation (2022):**
- Proposed TDM exception for commercial use (not implemented)
- Rights holder opt-out mechanism discussed
- Transparency obligations for AI developers

## 7. Remedies and Enforcement

### 7.1 Civil Remedies (CDPA 1988, ss 96-102)

**Available Remedies:**
1. **Injunction** (interim and final)
2. **Damages** or **account of profits** (election)
3. **Delivery up** of infringing articles
4. **Destruction or forfeiture**

**Damages Assessment:**
- *Phonographic Performance Ltd v Hagan* [2016] EWHC 3076 (IPEC)
- Compensatory principle; user principle (hypothetical licence fee)

### 7.2 Criminal Offences (CDPA 1988, ss 107-110)

Making, dealing, or facilitating infringing articles:
- Knowledge or reason to believe required
- Maximum penalty: 10 years' imprisonment and/or unlimited fine

### 7.3 Technological Protection Measures (CDPA 1988, ss 296-296ZF)

Circumvention of effective technological measures:
- Civil remedy (s 296ZD)
- Criminal offence for devices/services (s 296ZB)

## 8. Contractual Licensing

### 8.1 Legal Basis

**Freedom of Contract:** Parties may impose conditions beyond statutory rights
- *Watford Electronics Ltd v Sanderson CFL Ltd* [2001] EWCA Civ 317

**Palimpsest License as Contract:**
- Offer and acceptance through use
- Consideration: access to work
- Privity: licensee bound by terms

### 8.2 Restrictive Covenants

**Enforceability:**
- Must be reasonable in scope and duration
- Public policy considerations

**AI Training Restrictions:**
- Novel area; limited case law
- Analogous to database licence restrictions
- *British Horseracing Board Ltd v William Hill Organization Ltd* [2001] EWCA Civ 1268

### 8.3 Collective Licensing

**Licensing Bodies:** CDPA ss 116-144
- Copyright Licensing Agency (CLA)
- Performing Right Society (PRS)

**Extended Collective Licensing:** Not currently available in UK (EU mechanism)

## 9. Palimpsest-Specific Legal Analysis

### 9.1 Emotional Lineage Protection

**Legal Basis:**
- **Moral rights:** Integrity right (s 80)
- **Contract:** Licensee obligations
- **Cultural heritage:** Public interest considerations

**Enforcement Challenges:**
- Proving "derogatory treatment"
- Quantifying harm to reputation
- Cross-border jurisdiction

### 9.2 Quantum-Proof Traceability

**Legal Implications:**
- Evidential requirements (Civil Procedure Rules)
- Cryptographic signatures as evidence
- Future-proofing attribution mechanisms

**UK Evidence Law:**
- Electronic signatures admissible (Electronic Communications Act 2000)
- Blockchain evidence: *AA v Persons Unknown* [2019] EWHC 3556 (Comm)

### 9.3 DAO Governance

**Legal Personality:**
- DAOs not recognised legal entities in UK
- Likely unincorporated association or partnership
- Liability concerns for participants

**Contractual Framework:**
- Smart contracts as legally binding agreements
- *Executors of Cummings v Danks* [2020] EWHC 1273 (Comm)

### 9.4 Jurisdiction and Choice of Law

**Palimpsest Primary Jurisdiction:** Netherlands (Dutch law)

**UK Recognition:**
- Rome I Regulation (retained EU law): contractual obligations
- Rome II Regulation (retained EU law): non-contractual obligations
- Brussels Recast: jurisdiction (retained with modifications)

**Choice of Law Clause:**
- Parties may choose governing law (Rome I, Art 3)
- UK courts will generally respect choice of Netherlands law
- Mandatory UK law may override (e.g., consumer protection)

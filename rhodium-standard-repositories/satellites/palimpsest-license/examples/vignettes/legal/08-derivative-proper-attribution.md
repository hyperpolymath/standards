# Legal Vignette: Derivative Work with Proper Attribution

## Scenario: Respectful Derivative Creation with Full Palimpsest Compliance

**Original Work**: *Refugee Lullabies* (song collection)
**Original Creator**: Amara Osman (Somali-British musician)
**Derivative Creator**: The Harmony Collective (youth choir)
**Outcome**: Model compliance, collaborative derivative, mutual benefit

---

## The Story

Amara Osman created *Refugee Lullabies*: a collection of songs based on lullabies sung by mothers in refugee camps—Somali, Syrian, Afghan, Eritrean. She recorded oral histories, learned the melodies, and adapted them for contemporary audiences, always crediting the communities and specific women who'd shared their songs.

She licensed the collection under Palimpsest v0.4:

```markdown
## Refugee Lullabies - License & Attribution

**Musician**: Amara Osman
**Cultural Sources**: Somali, Syrian, Afghan, and Eritrean mothers in refugee camps (specific names included in each song's liner notes)
**License**: Palimpsest v0.4

### Emotional & Cultural Context

These are not my lullabies—they belong to mothers who sang to children in impossible circumstances. I am a steward, not the source.

### Appropriate Uses
- Educational contexts (teaching about refugee experiences, music traditions)
- Solidarity concerts and fundraisers for refugee causes
- Therapeutic settings (the songs carry healing intention)
- Derivative works that honour the mothers and their cultural contexts

### Prohibited Uses
- Commercial exploitation without revenue-sharing
- Stripping cultural context (turning these into "world music" commodities)
- AI training without consent
- Uses that aestheticize refugee trauma without honouring resilience

### Attribution Requirements

**Minimum:**
- Amara Osman (adapter/recorder)
- Original singer (if identified) or community (e.g., "Somali mothers at Dadaab camp")
- "From *Refugee Lullabies* collection, licensed under Palimpsest v0.4"

**Ideal:**
- Full liner notes with cultural context
- Links to refugee support organizations
- Acknowledgment that these songs carry emotional lineage from mothers in crisis
```

### The Derivative Work Request

The Harmony Collective, a youth choir in Manchester comprising refugee and British-born teens, discovered *Refugee Lullabies*. Their director, Sarah Mitchell, contacted Amara:

> "Dear Amara,
>
> Our choir includes teens from Syrian, Afghan, and Somali families. Many recognize these lullabies—their mothers sang them. We'd love to create choral arrangements, blending the original melodies with harmonies the teens compose.
>
> We understand your work is licensed under Palimpsest v0.4. We want to do this right. Can you guide us on proper attribution and cultural respect?
>
> Best,
> Sarah Mitchell, Harmony Collective"

### The Collaboration Process

Amara was moved by the request and proposed a collaborative approach:

**Step 1: Understanding intent**

Amara met with the choir (via Zoom). She asked:
- What do these lullabies mean to you?
- How do you want to honour the mothers who sang them?
- Will this be commercial or community-based?
- How will you preserve cultural context in your arrangements?

The teens shared stories: "My mum sang this in the camp before we came to the UK." "I barely remember, but hearing this brings back feeling safe." "I want to share this with British kids so they understand our families' strength."

**Step 2: Cultural consultation**

Amara connected the choir with some of the mothers she'd originally recorded (those willing and able). The mothers gave guidance:
- Preserve the core melody (it's not just notes—it carries cultural memory)
- Feel free to add harmonies (music evolves, and your voices matter)
- Include context: explain why mothers sang these songs, what they meant
- Share with your communities, not just audiences

One mother (anonymized for safety): "My daughter is your age. She needs to remember this song. You singing it keeps it alive."

**Step 3: Licensing terms**

Amara and Sarah negotiated derivative work terms:

**Agreement:**
1. **Attribution** (per Palimpsest requirements):
   - "Arranged by The Harmony Collective"
   - "Based on *Refugee Lullabies* by Amara Osman"
   - "Original lullabies from [specific communities]"
   - Full liner notes with cultural context and mothers' stories (anonymized where needed)

2. **Cultural context preservation**:
   - Each performance includes spoken introduction explaining the songs' origins
   - Program notes include refugee history
   - Choir commits to learning about the conflicts that displaced these families

3. **Revenue sharing** (if commercial):
   - Harmony Collective's performances were mostly free community concerts (no issue)
   - If they recorded an album or did paid concerts: 20% of revenue to refugee support organizations

4. **Derivative license**:
   - The choral arrangements would also be licensed under Palimpsest v0.4
   - Ensuring that future derivatives of the arrangements maintain cultural context

5. **AI & recording restrictions**:
   - Recordings cannot be used for AI voice synthesis or music generation training
   - Streaming platforms must preserve full attribution metadata

6. **Community accountability**:
   - Amara could attend performances (invited, not required)
   - Refugee community members get free tickets
   - Choir commits to ongoing relationship with refugee organizations

### The Performances

The Harmony Collective premiered their arrangements at a fundraiser for Refugee Action. The program notes read:

> **Programme: Songs of Home & Displacement**
>
> *Tonight's performance features lullabies sung by Somali, Syrian, Afghan, and Eritrean mothers in refugee camps, recorded and adapted by Amara Osman in her collection *Refugee Lullabies*. Our choir has created choral arrangements that honor these mothers' voices while adding harmonies composed by our refugee and British-born members.*
>
> **Attribution:**
> - Original lullabies: Mothers at Dadaab (Kenya), Zaatari (Jordan), and other camps (some names withheld for safety)
> - Collection & adaptation: Amara Osman
> - Choral arrangements: The Harmony Collective
> - License: Palimpsest v0.4
>
> **Cultural Context:**
> These are not performance pieces first—they are survival tools. Mothers sang these songs to comfort children in tents, waiting years for resettlement, grieving lost homes. We honour their strength and share their music as an act of memory and solidarity.
>
> **Proceeds:** All ticket sales benefit Refugee Action Manchester.

Amara attended. Mothers from the original recordings attended (those local and willing). Refugee teens performed their own histories. British-born teens learned their neighbours' stories through music.

One review: "The Harmony Collective's *Songs of Home & Displacement* is a masterclass in cultural respect. Every voice—original singers, adapter Amara Osman, and the young choir—is honored. This is how derivatives should be done."

The choir recorded their arrangements. Following Palimpsest requirements:


**Streaming platforms:**
- Spotify, Apple Music, etc. displayed full attribution
- Album description included cultural context
- Link to Refugee Action in artist bio
- Revenue split: 40% choir, 40% Amara, 20% refugee organizations

**AI protection:**
- Sarah contacted streaming platforms to opt recordings out of AI training datasets
- Included "No AI Training" flag in metadata
- Monitored for unauthorized use in music generation tools

## Legal Analysis

### Why This Compliance Worked

**1. Proactive Permission Request**
The choir contacted Amara before creating derivatives, showing good faith and enabling collaboration.

**2. Cultural Consultation**
They involved the original communities (mothers, refugee families), not just the legal copyright holder.

**3. Comprehensive Attribution**
Every level of creation was credited: original singers → Amara's adaptation → choir's arrangements.

**4. Context Preservation**
Cultural and emotional context was embedded in performances, recordings, and metadata—not an afterthought.

**5. Revenue Sharing**
While not legally required for all Palimpsest uses, the choir chose to share revenue as ethical practice.

**6. Derivative Licensing**
Licensing their arrangements under Palimpsest ensured protection would continue through future derivatives.

### Palimpsest Clauses Demonstrated

**Clause 2.3 (Metadata Preservation):**
Metadata in audio files, program notes, and streaming platforms preserved full attribution chain.

**Clause 3.1 (Derivative Works):**
Palimpsest permits derivatives that preserve cultural context and provide proper attribution. The choir exemplified this.

**Emotional Lineage Protection:**
The choir honored the mothers' original intent (comfort children, preserve culture) while adding their own voices.

**Community Co-ownership:**
Though Amara held legal copyright, she acknowledged community ownership and facilitated ongoing relationship.

## Practical Lessons

### For Derivative Creators

**Do:**
- Contact original creators before making derivatives
- Ask about cultural context and appropriate use
- Consult original communities when possible
- Preserve full attribution chain
- Embed cultural context in all formats
- License your derivative protectively (continue the Palimpsest chain)
- Offer revenue sharing as ethical practice
- Maintain ongoing relationship with original creators/communities

**Don't:**
- Assume "transformative" means "no attribution needed"
- Strip earlier creators from attribution to simplify
- Treat cultural context as optional
- Commercialize without considering ethics
- Ignore original creators after getting permission

### For Original Creators

**Do:**
- Respond thoughtfully to derivative requests
- Provide guidance on cultural respect
- Connect derivative creators with communities
- Celebrate respectful derivatives (they extend your work's reach)
- Model collaboration over control

**Don't:**
- Reflexively deny all derivative requests
- Demand excessive fees for community-based derivatives
- Withhold information that would help derivatives be respectful
- Forget that derivatives can serve your mission

### For Communities

**Your rights:**
- Even if not legal copyright holders, your cultural traditions deserve respect
- Palimpsest's framework supports community consultation
- You can advocate for proper representation

**Your role:**
- Provide cultural guidance to creators and derivative artists
- Celebrate works that honour your traditions
- Call out appropriation when you see it

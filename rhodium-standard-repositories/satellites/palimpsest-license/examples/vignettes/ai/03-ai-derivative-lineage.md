# AI-Specific Vignette: AI-Generated Derivative with Lineage

## Scenario: AI Creates Derivative Work, Preserving Cultural Lineage

**Original Work**: "Songs of the Borderlands" (Palimpsest-licensed poetry collection)
**AI Tool**: Claude (anthropic AI) with Palimpsest awareness
**User**: High school teacher creating educational materials
**Outcome**: Respectful AI-assisted derivative preserving emotional lineage

---

## The Scenario

**Background:**

Rosa Martinez, a high school English teacher in Arizona, teaches a unit on border literature and immigration narratives. She discovered "Songs of the Borderlands," a poetry collection by Maya Delgado, licensed under Palimpsest v0.4.

The collection features poems about Mexican-American border experiences: family separation, cultural duality, language loss, resistance.

**Challenge:**

Rosa wanted to create:
- Simplified versions for struggling readers
- Study guides with analysis questions
- Creative writing prompts inspired by the themes

But simply using AI to "rewrite" or "summarize" the poems would violate Palimpsest's emotional lineage protection—stripping the cultural weight and personal testimony.

**Solution:**

Use AI ethically as a *collaborator*, not an extractor, preserving attribution and cultural context.

---

## Ethical AI-Assisted Process

### Step 1: Check License Permissions

**Rosa reads the Palimpsest metadata:**

```markdown
## Songs of the Borderlands - Palimpsest License

**Poet**: Maya Delgado
**Cultural Context**: Mexican-American border experiences, family migration narratives
**License**: Palimpsest v0.4

### Appropriate Uses:
✅ Educational use (with proper attribution)
✅ Creating teaching materials
✅ AI-assisted analysis (preserving cultural context)
✅ Derivatives that honor emotional lineage

### Prohibited:
❌ Stripping cultural context
❌ AI rewrites that erase border politics
❌ "Simplification" that removes emotional weight
❌ Using without attribution to Maya Delgado

### AI Use:
- Non-interpretive (study guides, summaries): Allowed
- Interpretive (AI-written poems "in the style of"): Requires Maya's consent
- Educational AI assistance: Allowed if cultural context preserved
```

**Rosa's plan:** Create study materials WITH AI assistance, maintaining Maya's voice and attributing fully.

### Step 2: Contacting the Creator

Rosa emails Maya:

> Subject: Request to Use "Songs of the Borderlands" in Teaching (with AI assistance)
>
> Dear Maya,
>
> I'm a high school English teacher in Arizona. Your collection "Songs of the Borderlands" is exactly what my students need—many are themselves border kids navigating similar experiences.
>
> I'd like to create educational materials:
> - Simplified versions for struggling readers (preserving your voice, just easier vocabulary)
> - Study guides with discussion questions
> - Writing prompts inspired by your themes
>
> I plan to use AI (Claude) to help draft these, but I'll:
> - Keep your cultural context central
> - Attribute all materials to your original work
> - Review everything to ensure your voice isn't erased
> - Share final materials with you for approval
>
> Your license allows educational use. I'm contacting you out of respect and to ensure I do this right. May I proceed?
>
> Best,
> Rosa Martinez

**Maya responds:**

> Rosa,
>
> Thank you for asking. Yes, you may create these materials. I love that your students will see themselves in my work.
>
> Guidelines:
> 1. Always attribute to me and the Palimpsest license
> 2. Keep the border politics central—don't sanitize
> 3. If using AI, disclose that and review carefully
> 4. Send me the final materials—I'd love to see them
>
> I trust your judgment. These poems exist to serve kids like yours.
>
> Maya

### Step 3: AI-Assisted Creation (Preserving Lineage)

**Rosa uses Claude with careful prompting:**

**Example: Simplifying a Poem**

**Original Poem (by Maya):**
```
"Frontera Dreams"

We cross in whispers,
tongues tied between English hunger
and Spanish memory—
neither home anymore,
both ours to claim.

La Migra's shadow lengthens
but we dance in it,
two-stepping between
what they call illegal
and what we know is survival.
```

**Rosa's prompt to Claude:**

> I'm a teacher creating accessible versions of "Frontera Dreams" by Maya Delgado (Palimpsest-licensed border poetry) for struggling readers.
>
> **Requirements:**
> - Simplify vocabulary while preserving emotional weight
> - Keep cultural and political context (border crossing, La Migra, linguistic duality)
> - Maintain Maya's voice and perspective
> - Explain difficult concepts in footnotes, not by removing them
>
> **Don't:**
> - Sanitize the politics
> - Remove Spanish language elements
> - Make it "neutral" or generic
> - Erase the border experience
>
> Create an accessible version for 9th-grade reading level, preserving the above.

**Claude's response:**

```
"Frontera Dreams" (Accessible Version)
By Maya Delgado
Adapted for educational use by Rosa Martinez (with AI assistance)

We cross in quiet voices,¹
our tongues caught between
the English we need²
and the Spanish we remember—
neither language feels like home anymore,
but both belong to us.

La Migra's shadow³ grows long
but still we dance,
moving between
what they call "illegal"⁴
and what we know is staying alive.

Footnotes:
1. "Cross in whispers" - Many families cross the US-Mexico border quietly, at night, to avoid border patrol
2. "English hunger / Spanish memory" - The tension between needing English to survive in the US vs. Spanish being their family's language
3. "La Migra" - Spanish slang for US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)
4. "Illegal" - The poem critiques calling people "illegal" when they're simply trying to survive

**Cultural Context:**
This poem speaks to the experience of Mexican-American families navigating border politics. The poet (Maya Delgado) writes from personal experience of growing up on the borderlands.

**Discussion Questions:**
1. Why does the poet use both English and Spanish? What does this language-mixing represent?
2. What does "dancing in the shadow" of immigration enforcement mean?
3. How does the poem challenge the word "illegal"?

Attribution: Original poem "Frontera Dreams" from *Songs of the Borderlands* by Maya Delgado. Licensed under Palimpsest v0.4. Accessible adaptation by Rosa Martinez with AI assistance from Claude (Anthropic). Cultural context preserved with author's consent.
```

**Why this works:**
- ✅ Simplifies language without erasing meaning
- ✅ Preserves Spanish elements and explains them
- ✅ Keeps political context (La Migra, "illegal" critique)
- ✅ Adds educational scaffolding via footnotes
- ✅ Full attribution to Maya + disclosure of AI use
- ✅ Cultural context explicitly preserved

### Step 4: Review and Approval

Rosa sends the adapted materials to Maya.

**Maya's feedback:**

> Rosa,
>
> This is exactly right. You kept the heart while making it accessible. I especially love the footnotes—they teach without condescending.
>
> One change: In footnote 4, add "The poem uses 'illegal' to show how dehumanizing this language is. People can't be illegal—actions can be, but humans are always legal beings with dignity."
>
> Otherwise, perfect. Share this with your students. And tell them they can write to me—I answer all student letters.
>
> Maya

Rosa makes the change and uses the materials in class.

## Key Principles Demonstrated

### For AI-Assisted Derivatives

**Do:**
1. **Check license**: Understand Palimpsest permissions
2. **Contact creator**: Even if licensed, ask for guidance
3. **Preserve context**: Cultural and emotional lineage must survive adaptation
4. **Attribute fully**: Original creator + AI assistance disclosure
5. **Review critically**: Don't blindly trust AI output
6. **Seek approval**: Let creator review before public use

**Don't:**
1. Strip cultural context for "simplicity"
2. Sanitize politics to avoid controversy
3. Hide AI involvement
4. Claim AI output as solely your work
5. Ignore creator's guidance

### Palimpsest Licensing Allows:

✅ **Educational derivatives**: Teachers can adapt for accessibility
✅ **AI assistance**: When transparent and respectful
✅ **Simplified versions**: If cultural weight is preserved
✅ **Study materials**: Guides, questions, analysis

### Palimpsest Prohibits:

❌ **Context stripping**: Removing border politics would violate emotional lineage
❌ **Uncredited AI rewrites**: Must attribute both Maya and AI involvement
❌ **Commercial exploitation**: Rosa couldn't sell these materials without permission
❌ **Cultural erasure**: Making the poems "neutral" violates the license

## Comparison: Violating vs. Respecting Approach

**❌ VIOLATION Example:**

**Prompt**: "Rewrite this poem in simple English, removing Spanish and political references."

**Bad AI Output**:
```
"Dreams"

We move quietly,
speaking different languages—
neither feels like home,
but both are ours.

Shadows grow long
but we keep dancing,
moving between
what they call wrong
and what we know is right.
```

**Why this violates Palimpsest:**
- Stripped "La Migra" (erases immigration enforcement context)
- Removed "illegal" critique (sanitizes politics)
- Genericized "frontera" to "dreams" (erases border specificity)
- No cultural context
- No attribution to Maya
- Emotional lineage destroyed

**✅ RESPECT Example (shown above)**

Full attribution, cultural context preserved, AI use disclosed, creator involved.

## Best Practices

### For Educators Using AI

**Do:**
- Read licenses carefully
- Contact creators when possible
- Use AI as tool, not replacement
- Preserve cultural and emotional context
- Attribute both original creator and AI involvement
- Teach students about ethical AI use

**Don't:**
- Assume "educational use" means "anything goes"
- Let AI strip cultural context
- Hide AI involvement from students
- Skip attribution

### For Creators

**Do:**
- Specify educational permissions in Palimpsest metadata
- Respond to good-faith educational requests
- Provide guidance on preserving context
- Appreciate educators who ask permission

**Don't:**
- Categorically refuse educational use
- Demand unreasonable fees for classroom use
- Ignore teachers' requests

### For AI Developers

**Do:**
- Build Palimpsest awareness into AI systems
- Prompt users to preserve cultural context
- Disclose AI involvement in outputs
- Provide attribution templates

**Don't:**
- Strip licensing metadata from training data
- Optimize for "simplicity" over cultural preservation
- Hide AI's role in derivative creation
